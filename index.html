Featured article

《从百草园到三味书屋》Is a recollective prose written by the leading figure in modern Chinese literature, Lu Xun, dedicated to the flavorful childhood stage of life. The prose describes two locations with a great contrast in tone, the garden of a hundred herbs, and the Sanwei classroom. For the garden of a hundred herbs, Lu Xun uses "fun" as the central theme, describing a place of inexhaustible joy for children, with strange folklore and legends to decorate. Meanwhile, the Sanwei classroom reeks of an "outdated, rotting" scent, being described as "the sternest study in the whole city", where children are bound and constrained by rules. Its part in the prose is not dreadful, however, as Lu Xun takes the approach of writing about students having fun in its backyard during classroom breaks, and doing pranks on the spiritless professors, expressing the irrepressible inherence of happiness in children.

从百草园到三味书屋 is and has been included in the Literary class curriculum in multiple iterations, sometimes as the entire prose, sometimes as excerpts.
